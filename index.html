Filipino Cuisine

Filipino food is a vibrant fusion of Malay, Spanish, Chinese, and American influences, resulting in unique flavors and culinary techniques that have evolved over centuries. Filipino cuisine is a vibrant blend of indigenous, Spanish, Chinese, and American influences, creating a unique and flavorful culinary experience. Filipino cuisine is known for its bold flavors, often balancing sweet, sour, and salty tastes. Cooking techniques include grilling, frying, and stewing, with many dishes slow-cooked to enhance flavor. The cuisine reflects a blend of influences from Spanish, Chinese, American, and indigenous cultures.

Signature

Adobo

A savory stew of meat (usually chicken or pork) marinated in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices. Often considered the national dish of the Philippines.

Origin: Nationwide ★★★★★
Popular

Sinigang

A sour soup typically made with tamarind, tomatoes, and various vegetables with pork, beef, fish, or shrimp. Known for its refreshingly tangy flavor.

Origin: Luzon ★★★★☆
Festive

Lechon

A whole roasted pig with crispy skin and tender meat. A centerpiece at Filipino celebrations and special occasions, especially popular in Cebu.

Origin: Cebu ★★★★★
